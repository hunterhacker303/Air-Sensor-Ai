Air Quality Index

The air quality index (AQI) in major cities across India varies significantly, reflecting the diverse environmental challenges faced by each urban area. Factors such as industrial emissions, vehicular pollution, and construction activities contribute to fluctuating AQI levels, often leading to health advisories during periods of poor air quality. Cities like Delhi frequently experience high AQI readings, particularly during winter months when weather conditions exacerbate pollution levels. Monitoring these indices is crucial for public health and environmental policy, as they provide essential data for mitigating air pollution and improving overall air quality in urban settings.
